CS 639: Road Lane Detection

Anuj Amin & Prasun Guragain

Problem

The problem statement we are trying to solve is detecting traffic lanes in real-time. With cars being driven every day and engineers trying to make cars safer, many companies are turning to machine learning and self-driving in order to help solve this problem.

This is an essential problem in the real world as there are an increasing number of cars with built-in technologies to do this. For example, Tesla has self-driving cars, and many other big-name automotive companies have technologies such as lane assist, ensuring that the driver is driving in the correct lane and direction. However, this technology is still very new and has many problems. For example, how can the computer detect a lane in poor weather conditions or a faded-out lane? What if there are no street lights at night that prevent the computer from detecting any lanes? What about the effects of lane-like objects on the road such as railways and walkways? These variables affect every driver and can affect traffic lane detection as well. While existing algorithms can have a very high detection rate, generally most of these tests are done using favorable conditions such as a clear road and good lighting (Huang et. al, 2021).

Importance of Lane Detection

According to the article “Drowsy driving statistics and facts 2022” by Elizabeth Rivelli, there have been over 10,000 fatal crashes due to drowsy or careless driving, including failure to keep in the proper lane, being distracted (by phone, talking eating, etc), and swerving due to weather, objects, etc. These serious fatalities could be easily avoided by equipping programs in modern cars with good traffic lane detections that can account for any, or at least most, situations (Rivelli, 2022).

Dataset

We decided to use some of the images from the UNet Lanes v3 dataset in Kaggle. To save on computation costs, a part of this dataset is a subset of a the much larger Carla Self driving car lane dataset. Additionally, images have been augmented to have dashed lines labelled as mostly solid. The other part of the dataset comes from a variety of sources raning from dashcam videos to images.

Proposed Method

We first worked on creating a mask to highlight where the ptential lanes are, and then used Canny Edge Detection in order to make the edges apprear much more defined. We then worked on applying the Hough transform in order to detect edges. Then we applied the model and test on images in our dataset. However, without using a deep learning technique, the images needs to be high contrast and low harsh weather conditions. Therefore, we used a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to enhance our model to detect images in any conditions.
